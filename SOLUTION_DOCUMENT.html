1. Problem Statement & Relevance

The Problem

A student's day is scattered across too many places at once. Class timings live in one PDF, assignment deadlines show up in a WhatsApp group, placement notices arrive over email, and hostel updates sit on a notice board. Things fall through the cracks. Students miss submission dates, forget to register for drives, and hear about events only after they happen. The apps available today are mostly plain planners where you still have to type everything in by hand.

Why It Matters

India has close to 43 million students in higher education, and the number worldwide is over 250 million. Most of them check five or six different apps and groups every single day just to stay on top of things. Missing one message can cost a placement slot, a late fee, or a backlog. Over a semester that adds up to a lot of avoidable stress.

Theme Alignment

This is exactly what Problem Statement 1 is about. CampusFlow is an AI assistant that reads a student's routine, sums up the updates that matter, keeps the schedule in order, answers questions on the spot, and reminds them before something slips. The brief itself called it "Alexa for your college life", and that is the idea we set out to build.

What Makes This Different

  • It takes messy text, the kind you copy straight out of a WhatsApp group, and turns it into a clean list of deadlines and events on its own. No forms to fill.
  • It does not wait to be asked. It gives a short morning brief and flags clashes in your week before they become a problem.
  • It looks after academics and daily life together (mess menu, hostel notices, personal tasks), and you can simply talk to it.

2. Customer & Solution

Target Customer

Picture Aarav, a second year engineering student living in a hostel. He has six subjects, sits in three WhatsApp groups, helps run a club, and is just starting placement prep. He does not want yet another planner to fill in. He wants something that simply tells him what matters today and what is coming up next.

How We Solve It

CampusFlow takes all the scattered information a student deals with and turns it into one clear daily view. The main features are:

  • Routine understanding. Paste your timetable in any format and it builds your weekly routine.
  • Update summarization. Paste a WhatsApp or email dump and it gives you a short summary, then files the deadlines and events straight into your schedule.
  • Smart scheduling. A week calendar that also points out clashes, like a quiz landing on the same slot as a placement drive.
  • Morning brief and instant Q&A. A short daily brief with reminders, plus a chat you can use by typing or by voice.
  • Personal life in one place. Mess menu, hostel notices, and personal tasks all in a single feed.

User Workflow

1 · Capture
Add your timetable and paste any messy text from WhatsApp, email, or a notice.
2 · Organize
The AI reads it and turns it into a clean schedule and calendar.
3 · Act
You get reminders, clash alerts, and answers to anything you ask, by text or voice.

Working Prototype

The full flow is live and is walked through in the attached demo video: signing up, pasting a timetable, dropping a WhatsApp dump into the Inbox so it becomes a schedule, the week calendar catching real conflicts, the morning brief, and the voice assistant answering questions about your own schedule.

How the AI Works

  • The extractor reads free text and returns clean, typed JSON, with relative dates like "Friday 5pm" worked out against today. A forgiving parser handles small formatting slips from the model.
  • Answers in the assistant are built only from the student's own routine and items, so it stays grounded and does not invent things.
  • Conflict detection reads the weekly routine against dated items and reports real overlaps with a severity.
  • Passwords are hashed with bcrypt, and sessions are signed and verified in constant time.

Scaling

The whole app is serverless, so it scales up and back down on its own. DynamoDB runs on demand and partitions data per user, so there are no hot keys as the user base grows. Both the storage layer and the AI layer are swappable, so moving from a local file store to DynamoDB, or from one model to another, is a config change rather than a rewrite. As traffic grows, the morning brief can move to a scheduled job on Amazon EventBridge, and reads are cached at the edge through Amplify.

A note on Amazon Bedrock

We built this for Amazon Bedrock and the integration is in place. To actually call a Bedrock model, AWS needs a Marketplace subscription, and that needs a card on file. Our account only had UPI AutoPay, which AWS Marketplace does not accept, so the call was blocked. To keep the live demo working at no cost, the same converse() layer points at a free LLM instead. Moving back to Bedrock is a one file change plus an environment variable. Hosting on Amplify and data in DynamoDB stay on AWS either way.

4. Future Vision

Where This Goes

We want CampusFlow to become the everyday operating system for student life. The next big step is connecting directly to WhatsApp, Gmail, and the college portal so updates flow in on their own, with no copying and pasting. From there it can start acting for the student, registering for a drive, setting a reminder, or planning a week. Voice first, in more than one language, and always a step ahead.

Planned Improvements & Next Features

  • Direct integrations so updates auto import from WhatsApp, Gmail, and college portals.
  • Move the live AI to Amazon Bedrock with Claude, which the converse() layer already supports.
  • Photo capture, so a snap of a printed notice or timetable is read automatically.
  • Real push notifications through Amazon EventBridge with SNS or SES.
  • Hindi and other regional languages for both voice and text.
  • Wellness checks that spot an overloaded week and suggest a break.
  • Shared schedules for clubs and study groups, plus an offline friendly mobile app.

Roadmap

Multi Segment Expansion

The same engine, read messy updates, organize them, and help ahead of time, is not limited to students. It moves naturally from students to universities for admin and analytics, then to company onboarding where new joiners need a clear first ninety days, and on to communities and clubs that coordinate events and volunteers.

Value Impact

At scale this reaches millions of students, with each one saving roughly half an hour a day of chasing updates and far fewer missed deadlines. For a college it means higher engagement, fewer missed registrations and fee dates, and a simple way to reach every student personally without heavy infrastructure.
